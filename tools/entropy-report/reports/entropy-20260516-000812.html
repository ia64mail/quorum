The Volume-vs-Difficulty signal has shifted compared to the QRM6-close report. From #311 (QRM6 close, D=364.4) through #392 (QRM7 close, D=388.9), Volume grew 19% while Difficulty grew 6.7% — both gradients meaningfully steeper than the long QRM2–QRM5 plateau, where Volume could double with only single-digit Difficulty change. QRM7's character — many small fixes plus deep debugging cycles that surface as new modules (`InvocationResultStore`, `ContextSearchTraceLoggerService`, `cc-session-adapter`) rather than expansions of existing ones — explains the Volume growth. But the steeper Difficulty slope indicates that the new modules also reuse existing operands (transport types, session IDs, role enums, broker primitives) more than fresh feature areas like OpenSearch did. The N₂/η₂ ratio is climbing because the project is now layering on top of a settled vocabulary rather than introducing new domains. This is a healthy pattern in moderation; if Difficulty crosses 400 the threshold for genuine maintainability pressure becomes plausible.
